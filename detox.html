Emergency Detox Services

What Are Detox Services?

Detoxification (detox) services provide medical supervision and support during the withdrawal process from alcohol or drugs. These programs help individuals safely manage withdrawal symptoms while their body eliminates harmful substances.

Types of Detox Available

  • Medical Detox: 24/7 medical supervision with medication assistance
  • Inpatient Detox: Residential programs with comprehensive support
  • Outpatient Detox: Daily check-ins while living at home
  • Emergency Detox: Immediate crisis intervention services

Services Provided

  • Medically Supervised Withdrawal: Safe management of withdrawal symptoms with medical oversight
  • 24/7 Monitoring: Continuous health monitoring during the detox process
  • Medication Management: FDA-approved medications to reduce withdrawal symptoms
  • Crisis Intervention: Emergency services for acute withdrawal situations
  • Assessment and Evaluation: Comprehensive substance use evaluations
  • Stabilization: Physical and emotional stabilization before treatment
  • Treatment Planning: Coordination with ongoing treatment programs
  • Family Support: Education and support for family members

Who Can Benefit

Detox services are designed for individuals who:

  • Are experiencing physical dependence on alcohol or drugs
  • Need medical supervision during withdrawal
  • Have tried to quit on their own but experienced severe withdrawal symptoms
  • Are ready to begin recovery but need safe detox first
  • Require emergency intervention for dangerous withdrawal symptoms
  • Need stabilization before entering residential or outpatient treatment

What to Expect

Detox programs typically involve:

  • Initial Assessment: Medical and psychological evaluation
  • Medical Monitoring: Regular vital sign checks and health assessments
  • Symptom Management: Medication and comfort measures for withdrawal symptoms
  • Length of Stay: Typically 3-7 days, depending on substance and individual needs
  • Discharge Planning: Coordination with ongoing treatment providers
  • Aftercare Referrals: Connections to residential or outpatient programs

Specialized Programs Available

  • Alcohol Detox: Specialized protocols for alcohol withdrawal
  • Drug Detox: Programs for opioid, stimulant, and other drug withdrawal
  • Dual Diagnosis: Treatment for co-occurring mental health conditions
  • Youth Programs: Age-appropriate detox services for adolescents
  • Women's Services: Gender-specific detox programs
  • LGBTQ+ Affirming: Culturally competent care for LGBTQ+ individuals

24/7 Crisis Resources

National Suicide Prevention Lifeline: 988
SAMHSA National Helpline: 1-800-662-4357
Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741

Getting Started

Most detox programs accept walk-ins or have 24/7 admission services. Some may require:

  • Insurance verification (many accept Medicaid/Medicare)
  • Basic medical screening
  • Assessment of withdrawal risk
  • Emergency contact information

Important: Detox is often the first step in recovery. Many programs help coordinate ongoing treatment to support long-term sobriety after detox is complete.
