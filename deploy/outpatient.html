About Outpatient Treatment

Outpatient treatment programs provide flexible, comprehensive care for individuals dealing with substance abuse or mental health issues while allowing them to maintain their daily responsibilities and support systems.

Types of Outpatient Services

Regular Outpatient

Weekly individual or group therapy sessions with flexible scheduling options.

Intensive Outpatient (IOP)

More frequent sessions, typically 3-5 days per week for several hours each day.

Partial Hospitalization (PHP)

Day-long treatment programs that provide intensive care without overnight stays.

Medication Management

Psychiatric services for medication evaluation, monitoring, and adjustment.

Benefits of Outpatient Treatment

  • Maintain work, school, and family responsibilities
  • Stay connected to your support system
  • Apply treatment skills in real-world situations
  • More affordable than residential treatment
  • Flexible scheduling to fit your needs
  • Gradual transition back to independent living

Who Benefits from Outpatient Care

Outpatient treatment is ideal for individuals who:

  • Have completed residential treatment and need ongoing support
  • Have mild to moderate substance abuse or mental health issues
  • Have strong family or social support systems
  • Are motivated to maintain recovery while living at home
  • Need flexible treatment that accommodates work or school
